About Me

Hello, my name is Brian Shanken and I am an aspiring web developer. My background is a bit different from most in the field. I first ran into computing languages while pursuing my undergraduate degree in Physics. I had the opportunity to take a directed study focusing on plasma physics and using computational physics to work through real data with the University of Iowa. This was where I first learned the joy of communicating with a computer. The research eventually was published and I graduated from Valdosta State University.

Shortly after college, I was hired as a Business Analyst and found myself working with computers in a new way. This time, instead of researching for plasma to find new patterns, I got to use more commonplace data. My work mostly focused on using sequel databases and working with a custom CMS. I have switched from running simulations to pulling actionable insights out of large volumes of performance data.

My next challenge came in the form of customer-focused activity. I took a position as a Marketing Analyst in the B2B manufacturing field. We had the unique challenge of creating leads from being industry experts. Our motto was to deliver solutions and not sell products. I started in the position, as you would expect an analyst to perform, mostly collecting data on initiatives and then presenting the findings. Soon my role switched when I discovered the capabilities of our marketing software, Pardot, to build and host landing pages. This became a pivot point in my role. I was charged with developing and designing the landing pages for our ads and social media efforts. I challenged myself to increase the conversion rate and UX with each page I built. As my pages became more polished and I saw more success in my efforts, I realized I found my passion. I decided to pursue becoming a full web developer.

I am currently enrolled in a bootcamp program, through Georgia Tech, to learn all the skills I will need to perform at the top level in the field of web development. Feel free to look around my site and get a first-hand look at my new passion.
